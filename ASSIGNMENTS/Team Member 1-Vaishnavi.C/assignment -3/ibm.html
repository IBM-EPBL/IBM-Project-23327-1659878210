IBM

IBM offers three hardware platforms for cloud computing.[1] These platforms offer built-in support for virtualization. IBM also offers a virtualization application infrastructure, Websphere, which supports programming models and open standards for virtualization. The management layer of the IBM cloud framework includes IBM Tivoli middleware.[2] Management tools provide capabilities to regulate images with automated provisioning and de-provisioning, monitor operations and meter usage while tracking costs and allocating billing. The last layer of the framework provides integrated workload tools.[3] Workloads (in the context of cloud computing) are services or instances of code that can be executed to meet specific business needs.[4] IBM also offers tools for cloud based collaboration, development and testing, application development, analytics, business-to-business integration, and security.[5]
